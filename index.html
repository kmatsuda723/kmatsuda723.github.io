Profile

I am an Associate Professor at Keio University. My research interests are macroeconomics, labor economics, and the economics of higher education. I received my Ph.D. from Princeton University in 2019.

I study how universities and educational systems shape talent allocation and knowledge production, and how these mechanisms affect productivity, innovation, inequality, and mobility.
